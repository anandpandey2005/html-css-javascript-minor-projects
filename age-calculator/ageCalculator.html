What's The Culture Of Counting Years In Aging?

It's different in every corner of the world. But, since we are a Gregorian calendar, which is universally accepted and used by millions of people to calculate the age or number of days, you can rely upon this information.

Some cultures do not like to include the current year while determining a person's age. For example, in many Asian countries, people's age is calculated by excluding their current running age. If you've entered into 26th year, you will be 25 years old only and not as 26th.

Some cultures believe people get born at one age, and it might sound freaking, but there is a logic behind it.
